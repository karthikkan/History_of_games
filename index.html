Introduction

Video games have helped me grow into the person I am today. Through video games, I was able to truly appreciate the art of storytelling and worldbuilding. Because of video games, I learned to communicate with others, whether they were teammates or classmates. Video games inspired me to learn coding, helping me find the passion for it I have today. The First Games

SpaceWars is often considered the first computer game. It involves 2 players each controlling a spaceship trying to take the other one down. A gravity well acts as an external factor in pulling the ships together.

Demo: Spacewar

Controls: W, A, D to move. Space to shoot. Kill the enemy ship!

Pong

The online, scuffed version of real ping pong.

Controls: Move the mouse to play

Arcade Consoles etc

Space Invaders is developed by Tomohiro Nishikado and is an arcade shoot 'em up style game. The players controls a tank and fires bullets against a wave of aliens to beat the game.

Demo: Space Invaders

Controls: A, D to move. Space to shoot. Kill the enemy invaders!

Mario

In the 1980s, a new game that is the face of a world-renowned company and extends to multiple spin-offs would be released. The game is Mario, which used a 2d side scroller that revolutionized level design and video game making.

Controls: A, D to move. W to jump.

3D Graphics

During this time, the graphics in computers and consoles were getting continuously better, able to create a 3-dimensional experience. A great example of this was the original 3D first person shooter: Doom.

Online Gaming

With the rise of the internet, people began to connect online through video games. The video games of this time truly start to resemble the ones we see today.
